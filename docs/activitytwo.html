Day 13
Bad Ideas:

The Bad Ideas exercise is a powerful tool that promotes open-mindedness and encourages innovative thinking within teams. This exercise involves exploring seemingly wild and impractical proposals, deliberately seeking out ideas that may initially appear counter productive or unrealistic.

How it works:

  1. Set the timer for 3 minutes by clicking the start button.
  2. Get in groups of two or three people.
  3. Assign each group an objectively bad idea, like “Pickle-Flavored Lip Balm:” or “Onion-Flavored Toothpaste.”
  4. Give the groups five minutes to discuss all the potential benefits, uses, and selling points for their assigned product.
  5. Have each group “sell” the team on their bad idea.

Hint:

Have fun with it. Bad Ideas is a simple activity that can kickstart out-of-the-box thinking and help your team focus on benefits and possibilities rather than all the ways an idea could fail—which is the perfect mindset for generating exciting and original ideas during your formal brainstorming meeting.
